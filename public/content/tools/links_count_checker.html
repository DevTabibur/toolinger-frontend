What is a Links Count Checker and Why You Need It

Every website is made up of web pages, and every one of those web pages contains links. Some of them link to other pages on your website (external links), while others link to other pages on your site (internal links). A Links Count Checker is a software which navigates a webpage and tells you exactly how many of these links there are, and crucial details about them.

Understanding Link Counts

Consider links as the roads of the internet. Internal links enable visitors and search engines to move around easily between your pages, and external links take them to another website. You can measure these links to get an idea of the organization of your site and how tightly it is woven.

For example:

  • If you have few internal links, some pages will be "invisible" to search engines.
  • If you have too many external links, you might lose good traffic or "link juice."

Dofollow vs. Nofollow Links

Not all links are made equal. Dofollow links pass authority between sites, which helps with rankings. Nofollow links do not pass authority but are still beneficial for traffic and diversity. Our report maintains these separate so you know whether your website is too reliant on one or the other.

Internal vs. External Links

Internal links make it easier for search engines to explore your website and direct users to other sites. External links send users to other websites. They are both useful for SEO when kept in balance. Our tool shows you exactly how many of both you have, so you know if you're overusing or not using them.

FAQ

Why is it useful to know the number of links on a site?

Links are an important SEO consideration. They lead search engines and visitors around your site, assisting in building authority and relevance.

How does internal differ from external linking?

  • Internal links link one page on your site to another, keeping visitors interested and assisting search engines in crawling your material.

  • External links refer to links from your site to other sites that can contribute additional value to users and create credibility. Both are useful, but they play different functions in your SEO strategy.

Is excessive linking good for SEO?

No. Excessive linking can slow down visitors, dilute link strength, and make your pages look spammy to engines. A balanced and structured link architecture is critical for effective SEO.

What are dofollow and nofollow links?

  • Dofollow links pass SEO value (link juice) from one page to the next, boosting rankings.

  • Nofollow links do not pass link authority but can still drive traffic and add diversity to your link profile. Our tool tracks both clearly so you can make educated decisions.
